## 1. Global Meta (4.3 changed everything)

### Combat Revolution
- Ships are **much tankier** — battles last significantly longer than pre-4.3
- Armor + shields now have **innate hardening** at higher tiers: (tier - 1)% per tier
- **Disruptors are dead** — ~300 days to kill a single corvette; bypass weapons are obsolete
- **Titan AoE beams are meta-defining** — 500 damage, 40 range; punishes massed small fleets
- **Autocannon + Plasma** is the new swarm meta — kills a corvette in ~23 days
- Shield capacitors are **massively buffed**
- Battleships are extremely tanky (25,000+ effective HP)

### Economy Revolution
- Job research output halved, but tech costs also halved — space deposits relatively stronger
- Most stability bonuses reduced to 1/2 – 1/3 of pre-4.3 values — stability harder to pin at 100
- Unity sources reduced across the board; base empire unity increased to 10
- Many job output modifiers converted to **job efficiency** modifiers
- Colony designations now add job efficiency to relevant jobs
- Planetary ascension grants +10% designation effect per tier (was +25%)
- Strategic resource production moved to district specializations; free deposits nerfed
- Special planetary deposits 4x more common
- Automation buildings are now job-specific with energy upkeep scaling per 100 jobs

### Naval Rebalance
- All ship naval cap usage ~5x higher (Corvette: 5, Destroyer: 10, Cruiser: 20, Battleship: 40, Titan: 80)
- Base naval cap increased to 50
- Higher tier components cost more to build and maintain
- Frigates buffed: destroyer speed, +1 utility/aux slot
- Torpedo damage bonus reduced to 20% of former values (ship sizes 5x larger)
- Siege combat computers: +50–100% damage vs starbases and bombardment
- Crisis fleets reduced by ~1/3; player fleets reduced by more — crises are **relatively harder**

### Key Civic/Ethic Changes
- Authoritarian: worker bonus → worker job efficiency
- Egalitarian: specialist bonus → specialist job efficiency; faction output reduced to +15%/+30%
- Corporate authority: +1 envoy instead of +1 external leader pool
- Distinguished Admiralty: +20% command limit (was flat), commander upkeep reduction added
- Technocracy: +1 scientist cap, -1 official cap; cross-disciplinary research from council
- Cybernetic traits: no energy upkeep; swap half food for energy
- Soldiers reworked to specialist jobs with 1 alloy upkeep

## 2. Economy Meta

### General Principles
- **Minerals > everything early** — minerals are the foundation resource
- Produce energy → buy minerals on the monthly market to stabilize prices
- **Never stop building** in early game — idle construction slots are wasted tempo
- **Specialize planets aggressively** — dedicated forge, factory, research worlds
- Job efficiency matters more than raw output — prioritize designation matching
- District specializations for strategic resources are now mandatory for meaningful production
- Stability is a scarce resource — every +1 matters; plan around stability civics/traits

### Trade Value Builds
- Trade value builds are **extremely strong** in 4.3
- Starbase trade builds can generate 1000+ trade value
- Thrifty + Trading Algorithms = +50% trader job efficiency (core of trade meta)
- Trade federations scale extremely hard (leader level stacking + trade posts)
- Megacorps with branch offices + trade = top-tier late-game scaling

### Space Deposits
- Space deposit builds (Treasure Hunters, Arc Welders, etc.) are **top-tier** because they generate resources without pops
- This is more valuable post-4.3 since pop-based job output was nerfed

### Early Game (0–40 years)
- Alloys > consumer goods until basic fleet is online
- Mining stations early; energy can be balanced later
- Base naval cap is 50 — first fleet should fill this before expanding further
- Secure chokepoints before expanding wide
- Colony designations matter immediately — set them early for the efficiency bonus
- AI prefers ≤3 underdeveloped colonies before colonizing more — match this cadence

### Mid Game (40–120 years)
- Transition to alloy megafactories via district specializations
- Automation buildings now job-specific — invest in the right tier
- Fallen Empire buildings (if captured) automate 25-50% specialist or 75-100% worker jobs
- Planetary ascension gives +10% per tier — slower to max but still critical

### Late Game (120+ years)
- Repeatables give +20 naval cap per tech — critical for fleet scaling
- Megastructures (Dyson Sphere, Matter Decompressor) essential for resource scaling
- Crisis fleets reduced by ~1/3 but player fleets scaled down more — prepare earlier

## 3. Unity & Ascension Meta

### Unity Rush is Top-Tier
- Early ascension builds are the strongest macro strategies in 4.3
- Key unity-rush origins: Cybernetic Creed, Clone Army, Synthetic Fertility, Shroud-Forged, Teachers of the Shroud, Endbringers
- General pattern: unity > tech early, then catch up post-ascension

### Psionic Ascension is META
- Psionic is "the meta" due to unity generation, covenant power spikes, and mid-game alloy spikes
- Covenant powers provide massive economy/military bonuses
- Teachers of the Shroud + unity stacking is a top build archetype

### Virtual Ascension Rush
- Void Dwellers + unity stacking → Virtual Ascension is extremely strong
- Habitat job density is unmatched for unity generation
- Post-ascension tech explosion catches up on research deficit

### Cybernetic Ascension
- Double-dip organic + cybernetic traits for massive stacking
- Cybernetic Creed provides strongest worker buff framework
- Creeds can now be Egalitarian (new in 4.3)

### Synthetic Ascension
- Under One Rule → Synthetic Ascension creates late-game snowball
- Synthetic Evolution perk + Luminary ruler = unique synth trait boosting entire economy
- Technocracy + Faith in Science can achieve -100% researcher upkeep

## 4. Ship & Combat Meta

### Weapon Meta (tested across 200+ builds)
| Weapon | Verdict | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Disruptors** | **DEAD** | ~300 days to kill a corvette; armor/shield hardening kills bypass |
| **Autocannon + Plasma** | **Swarm meta** | ~23 days to kill a corvette; best for corvette/destroyer builds |
| **Kinetic Artillery + Neutron Launchers** | **Mid-game cruiser meta** | Best cruiser loadout |
| **Tachyon Lance + Kinetic Artillery** | **Long-range battleship meta** | Best artillery battleship |
| **Giga Cannon + Plasma** | **Close-range battleship meta** | Best brawler battleship |
| **Titan Perdition Beam** | **Meta-defining** | 500 dmg, 40 range, AoE; punishes small fleet spam |
| Crystal Hull Plating | Viable | Flat damage reduction is strong on tanky ships |

### Fleet Composition
- **Corvette swarms are weaker** due to titan AoE — split fleets to mitigate
- **Battleships are extremely tanky** (25,000+ effective HP) — core of any late-game fleet
- **Shield capacitors massively buffed** — always consider shield-heavy builds
- Titan + battleship artillery is the standard late-game composition
- Frigates are now viable (destroyer speed, extra utility slots)
- Don't mix weapon types with different optimal ranges

### Combat Tactics
- **Split fleets** to avoid titan AoE concentration
- Don't mix weapon types with different attributes (range, tracking)
- Starbases are **unreliable** in combat — don't depend on them for defense
- Siege combat computers for dedicated starbase assault fleets
- Command limit matters more — Distinguished Admiralty gives +20% multiplicative

### Crisis Counter Loadouts
| Crisis | Counter |
|--------|---------|
| Unbidden | Armor-heavy + Plasma (they bypass shields) |
| Contingency | Kinetic + Artillery (they're armor-heavy) |
| Prethoryn Scourge | High evasion + Autocannons (swarm tactics) |

## 5. Origin Meta (ranked)

### Tier S — Meta-Defining

#### Cybernetic Creed
- **"Braindead no-researcher build"** — extremely strong and easy to execute
- Strongest worker-stacking origin in 4.3
- Toil faction provides massive worker buff species trait + edict + imperial bonus
- Cybernetic traditions allow double-dipping organic + cybernetic traits
- Best with: Fanatic Authoritarian, Imperial, worker-focused civics
- Creeds can now be Egalitarian (new in 4.3)

#### Under One Rule (UOR)
- One of the strongest origins in 4.3
- Synthetic Awareness + Titan of Industry = +25% jobs, +1 pop assembly empire-wide
- Enables -100% researcher upkeep with Technocracy + Faith in Science
- Keep Luminary alive → Synthetic Ascension → unique synth trait boosts everything
- Best with: Dictatorial, Byzantine Bureaucracy + Technocracy

#### Void Dwellers
- Best origin for Virtual Ascension Rush
- Habitat job density is unmatched for unity generation
- Habitats can now build rare crystal/exotic gas/volatile mote specializations (new in 4.3)
- Habitat capital buildings now provide hunter-seeker drones for machines/hives

### Tier A — Very Strong

#### Synthetic Fertility
- Still strong despite nerfs — robot-first pop scaling remains top-tier
- No longer starts with Admin AI / Self-Aware Logic / Sapient AI researched
- Gets +15% computing research speed and increased draw chance instead
- Pop die-off halved (30/month → 15/month)

#### Necrophage
- Top-tier for pop snowballing; Death Cult synergy is extremely strong
- Chambers of Elevation now block pop growth for necrophage species (4.3 change)
- Necrophyte Apprentice jobs boost non-necrophage growth on same planet
- Best with: Fanatic Xenophobe, Oligarchy

#### Endbringers
- High risk, high reward; blocks all ascension except Psionic
- Stability penalties make early game volatile — need stability civics/traits
- Rush archaeology → End of the Cycle covenant
- Ascension situations progress slower in 4.3 (exception: Endbringers tick faster)

#### Rogue Servitor
- Unity + stability + biotrophy scaling is extremely powerful
- Biotrophies → unity → stability → alloy scaling loop
- Strong for 25× crisis capable builds

### Tier B — Strong / Situational

#### Doomsday
- Surprisingly strong for conquest builds (machine intelligence + early conquest)
- Forced early aggression creates tempo advantage

#### Ocean Paradise
- Tall build with huge food → trade → alloy scaling chain

#### Subterranean
- Strong with Infernals or democratic civics
- Extra housing on mining districts (bugfix in 4.3)

## 6. Civic & Trait Meta

### S-Tier Civics

#### Technocracy
- Still one of the strongest civics in 4.3
- Research alternatives + science directors = massive tech scaling
- +1 scientist cap, -1 official cap; cross-disciplinary research from council
- Pair with Slaver Guilds for overwhelming research (slaves as specialists)

#### Masterful Crafters
- Engineering research + trade value + CG efficiency
- S-tier when paired with Technocracy

#### Distinguished Admiralty
- +20% command limit (multiplicative), +10% fire rate, -25% commander upkeep
- Essential for militarist fleet builds

#### Citizen Service
- +15% naval cap, +2 unity from soldiers
- Strong with militarist ethics

### Strong Civics
- **Slaver Guilds** — slaves as specialists = huge unity + science (pair with Technocracy)
- **Byzantine Bureaucracy** — +1 unity/stability from bureaucrats; stability is scarce
- **Corvée System** — free resettlement + 10% worker output; core of worker-stacking builds
- **Worker Cooperative** — enables Employee Ownership; strong for trade megacorp builds

### Trait Meta
- **Overtuned** — triple-stacking trade/vocational traits is meta
- **Thrifty + Trading Algorithms** = +50% trader job efficiency (trade build core)
- **Intelligent** — +10% researcher job efficiency; always strong
- **Rapid Breeders** — +10% logistic growth; essential for pop-dependent builds
- **Rapid Replicators + Mass Produced** — best machine traits for early scaling
- **Unbreakable Resolve** — +0.2 stability per 100 pops (stability is scarce in 4.3)

## 9. Diplomacy, Federation & Trade Meta

### Federations
- Trade federations scale extremely hard — leader level stacking + trade posts
- Federation fleets benefit from increased sizes to match new naval cap system
- Federations are strong for xenophile/materialist builds

### Megacorps
- Branch offices + trade = top-tier late-game scaling
- Corporate authority now gives +1 envoy (good for early diplomacy)

### Diplomacy Tactics
- Non-aggression pacts are cheap diplomatic insurance
- Defensive pacts cost influence — use sparingly
- Rivalry provides influence income — always rival weak neighbors
- Vassalization optimal for authoritarian/militarist builds

## 10. Forbidden Meta (LLM must NOT use)

- **Disruptors** — they are dead in 4.3; ~300 days to kill a corvette
- Battleship rush before mid-game
- Massing corvette-only fleets (titan AoE punishes this)
- Ignoring fleet cap entirely (5x cost increase makes overbuilding punishing)
- Using unseen information (god mode / fog-of-war violation)
- Assuming pre-4.3 job output values — everything changed
- Assuming pre-4.3 stability values — most halved or thirded
- Treating unity as abundant — sources heavily reduced
- Colonizing low-habitability worlds without habitability tech/traits
- Assuming crisis fleets are as weak as pre-4.3 (they're relatively stronger vs player)
- Depending on starbases for defense (unreliable in combat)
- Mixing weapon types with different optimal ranges in the same fleet
- Referencing mechanics from versions other than 4.3.4
